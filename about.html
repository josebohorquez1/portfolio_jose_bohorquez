About Me

I am an aspiring software engineer with a background in computer science and communication studies, focused on building accessible, reliable, and purpose-driven software.

Background

I earned a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science with a minor in Communication Studies from the University of Florida. My academic work emphasized software design, problem solving, and logic, alongside clear technical communication.

Through coursework and projects, I developed experience with front-end and back-end concepts, including data structures, programming language design, and standards-based web development.

How I Approach Software Engineering

I approach software engineering with a strong emphasis on clarity, maintainability, and accessibility. I value code that is readable, well-structured, and built with real users in mind.

I am particularly interested in building software that solves meaningful problems and has a positive real-world impact, especially in contexts where usability and inclusion matter.

Accessibility and Advocacy

In addition to my technical work, I am actively involved in advocacy at the intersection of disability and technology through the National Federation of the Blind.

Through this work, I champion issues that directly affect blind users, including advancing better accessibility across software, applications, programs, and websites. I engage with accessibility standards, usability considerations, and real-world user needs to help promote technology that is usable by everyone.

This advocacy experience strongly informs how I approach software engineering, reinforcing the idea that accessibility is not a feature to add later, but a fundamental aspect of quality, user-centered design.

What’s Next

I am seeking opportunities where I can continue to grow as a software engineer while contributing to products and teams that value thoughtful design, accessibility, and impact.
